Internet Privacy

Warning: Lots of text to read :|

What is Internet Privacy?

Internet Privacy involves the right of personal privacy concerning the storing, reusing, and displaying of information of oneself in the Internet. Simply defined: all the data pertaining to us that is stored within the internet, who owns it?

Wait... We're Being Tracked?

Companies are specifically hired to monitor or create algorithms that track our movement as we traverse the internet. More likely than not, your browsing history and your movements on this web page are also being tracked. The reason companies track users is because they want to create a experience that is more specific to the individual. Ads that appear will being to cater to you specifically or two individuals doing the same exact search on a search engine may populate different results. However, the issue is that not many individuals who use the internet understand that all this is going on.

How We're Being Tracked

There are a plethora of ways in how companies keep track of us. A few prominent ones will be summarized here.

Cookies: Cookies are data stored by the user's browser; saved locally on their hard drive. They provide automation of certain features within a website such as remembering saved passwords. Also they record browsing habits which can be used to recommend sites and advertisements that caters to an individual. Finally, third party cookies that not associated with the main web page can save all the same information and data; thus leading to the risk of losing privacy.

Search Engines: Search engine such as Google and Yahoo all have the capability to track a users search and search history. Information such as type of computer, search pattern, IP address, and location are all used to make the search engine personalized. Searched shown typically remember previous information.

Internet Service Providers (ISP): All data transmitted by the user, the ISP has the ability to monitor it. The ISP has the potential to prevent any user's activities that they deem inappropriate based on their terms of service. However anything encrypted will be withheld from the ISP but the sender and recipient will still be known to the ISP.

tl;dr: Sites use cookies to track your movement on web pages. Search engines keep a search history to provide a better search result. Internet service providers monitor what you do on the internet.
